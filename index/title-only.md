Spectral Zeta Moments and Carleman Indeterminacy in Four-Dimensional Gauge Theory
Abstract.
 We identify a fundamental obstruction to a class of spectral approaches to the Yang–Mills mass gap by recasting the problem as a Stieltjes moment problem for logarithmic spectral data. In four spacetime dimensions, the associated moment growth is incompatible with Carleman determinacy, so the moment sequence does not uniquely determine the underlying spectral measure. As a consequence, distinct spectral configurations may share the same zeta-derived moment data, including configurations with and without a mass gap. This shows that moment-theoretic information extracted from spectral zeta derivatives is insufficient, by itself, to resolve the mass-gap question in four-dimensional gauge theory.
TDA Persistence Homology Applied to Spectral Gap Detection: A Reformulation of the Yang-Mills Mass Gap

Abstract.
We establish an equivalence between the Yang-Mills mass gap problem and a persistence condition in topological data analysis. Specifically, we prove that the existence of a spectral gap m > 0 in the Yang-Mills Hamiltonian is equivalent to infinite H₀ persistence of the vacuum eigenvalue under the natural energy filtration of spec(H), and further equivalent to nonvanishing of the Borel transform on its convergence contour. This four-way equivalence — spectral gap, TDA persistence, Borel envelope positivity, and a Rouché-type inequality — provides a geometric reformulation of the mass gap condition that does not appear in the existing Yang-Mills or resurgence literature. As a consequence, the open problem is reduced to a single verifiable inequality on the correction term of the Borel transform, with an explicit critical coupling g²_crit identified.
Quasiperiodic Structure on the Critical Line of Spectral Zeta Functions: Log-Spectral Encoding of Eigenvalue Gaps
Alt title: Zeta-Regularized Spectral Potentials from Integer Spectra and Correspondence with Tachyon Condensation
Abstract.
 We study the spectral zeta function on the critical line as a quasiperiodic signal generated by the logarithmic distribution of eigenvalues. In this picture, the spectral data admit a frequency-domain interpretation in which the presence of a mass gap is reflected by a dominant low-lying mode separated from the remainder of the spectrum. The resulting formulation yields an explicit lower bound for the critical-line amplitude in the regime where the first eigenvalue dominates the spectral sum. This recasts gap detection as a problem in harmonic analysis on logarithmic spectral data, and highlights a new bridge between operator theory, signal structure, and spectral geometry.
Topological Duality in the Goldbach Problem: Persistent Homology of the Prime Pair Generating Function
We establish that a Goldbach counterexample at an even integer 2m₀ is equivalent, in the language of topological data analysis, to a simultaneous event in two persistence diagrams: an infinite H₀ feature at 2m₀ in the position-space coverage filtration, and the death of the H₁ feature of the prime exponential sum at filtration parameter zero in the frequency-space representation. This duality — a single arithmetic failure manifesting as complementary topological events in position and frequency space — is new and does not appear in the Goldbach or TDA literature. As a consequence, the conjecture is reformulated as a joint persistence condition, and the existing frontier/velocity argument for large-scale coverage is identified as proving finite average H₀ persistence but not pointwise nonvanishing.
Borel Resummation and Complex-Analytic Stability Bounds in Non-Perturbative Quantum Field Theory
Abstract.
 We study the analytic continuation of divergent perturbative series through the lens of Borel regularization and complex stability bounds. The leading contribution defines a nonvanishing analytic envelope, while the remaining correction term determines whether the continuation remains stable on the relevant contour. This yields a sharp criterion separating the controlled regime from the unresolved regime and identifies a critical coupling beyond which the nonperturbative question remains open. The result situates the mass gap problem within a broader analytic framework connecting divergent series, contour stability, and regularized summation phenomena.
The Instanton Trans-Series as a Generational Decomposition: A Structural Isomorphism in Resurgence Theory
Abstract.
Resurgence theory expresses the Yang-Mills partition function as a trans-series indexed by instanton number k. We identify a structural isomorphism between this instanton decomposition and a generational prime decomposition framework in additive number theory, in which the instanton sector of charge k corresponds to the k-th prime generation, the instanton action kS₀ corresponds to the frontier bound, and the resurgence cancellation condition corresponds to the nonvanishing of a prime exponential sum. This isomorphism implies that the Yang-Mills mass gap and the Goldbach conjecture are instances of the same analytic problem — controlling a divergent generating function whose non-perturbative content is encoded in a sparse singularity set. The correspondence does not exist in either the resurgence or the number theory literature, and suggests a class of tools from each field applicable to the other.
GOLDBACH
Anti-Symmetry of the Lacunary Prime Series and a Parity Constraint on the Goldbach Winding Number
Abstract.
The generating function of odd primes on the unit circle satisfies an exact anti-symmetry under half-rotation, as a consequence of 2 being the unique even prime. We prove this anti-symmetry rigorously and derive three immediate consequences: zeros of the prime exponential sum are constrained to appear in antipodal pairs, the winding number of the sum around the origin is necessarily an even integer, and the domain of the nonvanishing problem is reduced by half. A bootstrapping bound is proved: if the minimum modulus of the N-prime partial sum exceeds unity, the addition of the next prime cannot introduce a zero. These results provide the sharpest known structural constraints on the zero set of the prime exponential polynomial and reduce the Goldbach nonvanishing problem to a quantitative lower bound on this minimum modulus.

The Goldbach Generating Function as a Dirichlet Square and the Natural Boundary Obstruction
The Goldbach pair-count Dirichlet series admits an exact factorisation as the square of the prime zeta function, which in turn has an explicit representation via Möbius inversion through the Riemann zeta function. We study the analytic consequences of this factorisation, identifying the natural boundary of the prime zeta function at the critical line as the precise obstruction to extracting a finite regularised value. The regularised value of the generating function at the relevant singular point — is shown to be approximately 0.0325 but unreachable by standard analytic continuation, and its unreachability is proved equivalent to the conjecture itself. This places the Goldbach problem in the same class as Ramanujan summation, with the critical difference that no closed form exists for the prime series, making the analytic continuation problem genuinely harder.
Basin Conditions for the Prime Zeta Dynamical System and a Fractal Reformulation of Goldbach's Conjecture
Applying basin-boundary analysis to the iteration of the prime zeta function squared, we derive an explicit analytic inequality — a condition on the product of the prime zeta function and its derivative on the critical line — whose satisfaction is equivalent to the Goldbach conjecture for the corresponding class of even integers. The inequality is derived by reverse-engineering the fractal attractor basin of the dynamical system, identifying the basin boundary derivative condition as the operative inequality rather than direct nonvanishing of the generating function. This reformulation converts Goldbach from an additive number theory statement into a dynamical systems problem on the critical line, and the derived inequality is the first explicit analytic condition of this type in the Goldbach literature. The approach further identifies the local basin condition — that each Goldbach-relevant point on the critical line lies within the basin — as more tractable than the global nonvanishing problem.

Reduced-Order and Spectral-Geometric Diagnostics of Protein Conformational Transitions
Abstract
We study protein conformational change through a unified reduced-order framework for folding, misfolding, and structural failure. The model replaces exhaustive molecular sampling with a compact nonlinear evolution law that detects transition thresholds directly in state space, separating stable, metastable, and unstable regimes. In parallel, we characterize the same conformational transitions through spectral and geometric features of high-frequency molecular signals, enabling rapid biosensing of decoherence and loss of structural integrity. The resulting framework links protein folding dynamics, singularity-based instability, phase-sensitive invariants, and real-time detection in a form relevant to neurodegenerative pathology, synthetic biology, and molecular informatics.
